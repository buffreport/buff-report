Advertising — Google AdSense

Six ad placement zones are reserved throughout the page. All are built to Google AdSense standard sizes. Placeholders will be swapped for <ins class="adsbygoogle"> tags when AdSense goes live.

#PlacementSizeCSS ClassPosition / Rationale
1 Top Leaderboard 728 × 90 .ad-leaderboard-top Above everything — 100% of visitors see this. Highest CPM placement.
2 Billboard Banner 970 × 90 .ad-slot (banner wrap) Below header, above nav. Maximum above-fold presence.
3 In-Feed Leaderboard 728 × 90 .ad-slot (infeed wrap) Between article sections. Captures engaged mid-page readers.
4 Sidebar Medium Rect 1 300 × 250 .ad-sidebar Top of right sidebar. Highest sidebar viewability.
5 Sidebar Medium Rect 2 300 × 250 .ad-sidebar Mid-sidebar. Second impression for engaged readers.
6 Pre-Footer Billboard 970 × 90 .ad-slot (prefooter wrap) Bottom of page. Captures scroll-through readers before exit.

Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do
  • Use #CFB87C for all gold elements
  • Use Playfair Display for editorial headlines only
  • Use Inter for all UI, nav, and body text
  • Keep accent colors at small sizes (pills, dots, badges)
  • Pin featured stories with a gold left border + #fffdf5 background
  • Link every article card to the original source
  • Update the hero story when a major story breaks
  • Use the existing CSS variable names throughout
  • Test layout at 320px, 768px, and 1200px widths
  • Keep AdSense slots at exactly the defined 6 positions
✗ Don't
  • Substitute CU Gold with yellow or amber
  • Use Playfair Display for UI labels or buttons
  • Mix in additional fonts beyond Inter and Playfair
  • Use accent colors for large background areas
  • Use bold/italic randomly to draw attention
  • Use placeholder # links in production
  • Leave a stale hero story for more than 24 hours
  • Hard-code hex values directly in HTML (use variables)
  • Build only for desktop
  • Add ad slots without reviewing AdSense policy
