About The Buff Report

Every headline. One page.

The Buff Report is an independent publication and is NOT affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by the University of Colorado or CU Athletics.

Our Mission

The Buff Report exists because following CU Buffs news means juggling a dozen different tabs, feeds, and publications. We built this site to fix that.

Our mission is simple: be the one-stop aggregator for CU Buffs fans. That means sports coverage, recruiting news, campus happenings, and Boulder community stories, all in one place, updated continuously. No opinion takes. No hot takes. Just the headlines that matter, sourced from the outlets doing the original reporting.

Who Made This?

The Buff Report was created by a CU fan, for CU fans. This is not a newsroom. We are not journalists breaking original stories. We are aggregators — we find the best coverage from around the web and surface it in a clean, readable format so you can stay informed without the tab sprawl.

We have deep respect for the reporters and publications who cover CU and Boulder. Every headline links directly back to the original source. We want to send you there.

What We Cover

The Buff Report aggregates headlines and links across four main categories:

  • Sports — Football, basketball, soccer, and all CU Buffs athletics
  • Recruiting — Transfer portal moves, commitments, and class rankings
  • Campus — University of Colorado news, academics, and student life
  • Around Town — Boulder community, local events, and Colorado stories relevant to the CU community

Aggregation Philosophy

All content — articles, reporting, photography, and original analysis — belongs to the respective publications and journalists who created it. The Buff Report displays only headlines and brief summaries (where available via RSS or API), and every link takes you directly to the original source.

If you are a publisher and have questions about how your content is displayed, please reach out at admin@buffreport.com.

Contact & Feedback

We are always looking to improve. If you spot a bad link, want to suggest a source we should add, or have a tip for a story, head to our contact page or story tips page.
